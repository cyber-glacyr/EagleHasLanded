WHY DOES IT MATTER?

Climate Change

Solar power produces less life-cycle greenhouse gas emissions than traditional fossil fuel energy. In the United States, more than one third of greenhouse gas emissions come from burning fossil fuels for electricity in buildings and homes. Just a single solar panel can reduce carbon dioxide emissions by more than 100 tons over its lifetime.

Solar Energy

Solar energy is renewable and carbon-free, so it is used in the fight against climate change. It generates energy by using solar panels, also called PV panels, to capture the Sunâ€™s energy. Solar panels, unlike any other energy source can be used on a home by home basis. This means that its adoption is key to fighting climate change.

Awareness

Solar energy today is often perceived as a more expensive alternative to fossil fuels in order to help the environment. However, Solar energy can reduce monthly electricity bills and give the ability to store electricity to use later (during an outage for example). Solar energy is a one time flat fee, and having a battery allows you to be unaffected by outages.

OUR STAKE

Houston, TX is a part of the coastal region of the United States. Over 40% of the U.S. population live in coastal areas and are facing the impact of climate change. In recent years, the Gulf Coast region has been severely impacted by natural disasters that have led to loss of life and damage to infrastructure. Home solar panels offer an avenue for power after natural disasters when the grid is vulnerable or damaged and cuts back on the demand for fossil fuels through other energy sources.
